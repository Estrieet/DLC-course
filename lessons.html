Beginner Lessons

Easy computer lessons with simple language and clear steps.

Computer Basics

Learn the parts of a computer, the screen, keyboard, and mouse.

  1. Look at the screen and keyboard.
  2. Press the power button to start.
  3. Move the mouse to see the pointer.

Practice: Move the mouse and click a button.

Homework: Find the power button on your device.

Using a Mouse

Move the mouse slowly. Click once to open.

  1. Rest your hand on the mouse.
  2. Move the mouse to move the pointer.
  3. Click the left button to select.

Practice: Click the button below.

Homework: Practice clicking on items on the screen.

Using a Keyboard

Use the keyboard to type letters, numbers, and short words.

  1. Place your hands on the keyboard.
  2. Press the keys gently.
  3. Type your name in a note app.

Practice: Type a short message.

Homework: Write one sentence about your day.

Opening Programs

Programs are apps you open on the computer.

  1. Find the program icon.
  2. Click or tap it once.
  3. Wait for the program to open.

Practice: Open your browser app.

Homework: Try opening a second app.

Saving Files

Save your work so it is still there later.

  1. Click Save or Save As.
  2. Choose a simple name.
  3. Press the save button.

Practice: Save a short note.

Homework: Open the saved note again.

Internet Basics

Use the internet to look up simple information and news.

  1. Open the browser app.
  2. Type in a website address or search word.
  3. Read the page slowly.

Practice: Search for a recipe or hobby.

Homework: Find one new fact online.

Email Basics

Email lets you send messages to friends and family.

  1. Open your email app.
  2. Click Compose or New Email.
  3. Type a simple note and send it.

Practice: Write a message to yourself.

Homework: Read one email safely.

Online Safety

Keep your information safe by being careful online.

  1. Only open emails from people you know.
  2. Do not share passwords.
  3. Look for locks in the browser address bar.

Practice: Check a website has a lock icon.

Homework: Ask someone before sharing personal details.

Passwords

Use a strong password to keep accounts safe.

  1. Choose a long phrase you can remember.
  2. Do not share it with others.
  3. Write it down in a safe place if needed.

Practice: Create a strong password idea.

Homework: Change one weak password.

Viruses

Computer viruses can slow your device and cause problems.

  1. Do not open unknown email attachments.
  2. Do not click strange links.
  3. Ask for help if you are unsure.

Practice: Look for unusual emails or popups.

Homework: Talk with a friend or teacher before opening unknown files.

Printing

Printing makes a paper copy of what is on the screen.

  1. Choose Print from the menu.
  2. Select the printer.
  3. Click Print.

Practice: Print a short note or image.

Homework: Check the paper output is clear.

Using USB Drives

USB drives store files you can move between devices.

  1. Plug the USB drive into the computer.
  2. Open the file on the drive.
  3. Save or copy files to the drive.

Practice: Save a picture or note to the USB drive.

Homework: Remove the drive safely.

Mobile Phones

Use a mobile phone to call, text, and access simple apps.

  1. Press the home button or swipe up.
  2. Tap the app you need.
  3. Use the keyboard on the screen to type.

Practice: Open a beginner app and explore.

Homework: Send a short message to a friend.

Cloud Storage

Cloud storage keeps files safe on the internet.

  1. Open a cloud storage app.
  2. Upload a simple file.
  3. Open the file again from the cloud.

Practice: Save one file to the cloud.

Homework: Check the file from another device if possible.

Troubleshooting

If something does not work, stay calm and try simple steps.

  1. Close the app and open it again.
  2. Check the power or cable connection.
  3. Ask for help if it still does not work.

Practice: Restart a small app if it freezes.

Homework: Write one step to try first when something stops working.

Those are the first lessons. Come back anytime to learn more.
